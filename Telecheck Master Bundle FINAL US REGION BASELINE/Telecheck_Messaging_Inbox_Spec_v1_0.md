## 1. Purpose

This specification defines the in-app messaging system: message types, threading model, safety triggers, relationship to external notifications, and the patient inbox. Messaging is critical-path (Master PRD §11.4) — it is the persistent communication layer between patients, clinicians, pharmacy, support, and AI.

---

## 2. Message types

| Type | Participants | Initiated by | Clinical safety | Retention |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `clinical` | Patient ↔ Clinician | Either party | Crisis detection active | Category A |
| `pharmacy` | Patient ↔ Pharmacist | Either party | Crisis detection active | Category C |
| `support` | Patient ↔ Support agent | Patient | Crisis detection active | Category C |
| `ai_conversation` | Patient ↔ AI Mode 1 | Patient | Full guardrail template governs | Category C (A for crisis/escalation) |
| `system` | System → Patient | System | Not applicable | Category C |
| `care_team` | Patient → Care team (broadcast) | Patient | Crisis detection active | Category A |

### System messages

System messages are generated by the platform, not by a human or AI. Examples: refill status updates, lab results ready, appointment reminders, delivery tracking. System messages are **not conversations** — they are one-directional notifications with a persistent record in the inbox. The patient cannot reply to a system message (but can tap to navigate to the relevant feature).

---

## 3. Threading model

### 3.1 Thread structure

Messages are organized into threads. A thread belongs to one patient and has one type. Thread types:

| Thread type | Thread lifecycle | Max active threads |
|---|---|---|
| Consult thread | Created when consult is booked; closed when consult is completed or cancelled | 1 per active consult |
| Refill thread | Created when refill is initiated; closed when refill is completed | 1 per active refill |
| Program thread | Persistent per program enrollment; used for ongoing care communication | 1 per program |
| AI conversation | Created per Mode 1 session; closed when session ends | 1 active at a time |
| Support thread | Created when patient contacts support; closed by support agent | 1 active at a time |
| System notifications | Not threaded — individual messages in the inbox | Unlimited |

### 3.2 Thread behavior

- Messages within a thread are ordered chronologically
- New messages in a thread are highlighted with an unread indicator
- Closed threads are archived but visible in thread history
- A patient can reference a closed thread when opening a new support thread ("I had an issue with refill #XYZ")

## 4. Safety triggers

Crisis detection (I-019) is active across all message types except system notifications. Specific rules:

| Trigger | Detection method | Response |
|---|---|---|
| Suicidal ideation keywords | Pattern matching + AI classification | Immediate crisis protocol: display helpline, notify care team, log Category A audit |
| Self-harm indicators | AI classification | Same as above |
| Abuse disclosure | AI classification | Modified crisis protocol: display support resources, notify designated safeguarding contact |
| Medical emergency language | Pattern matching + AI classification | Display emergency number, offer to connect to emergency services |

### Clinical messaging safety

When a patient sends a clinical message that indicates a potential emergency (e.g., "I'm having chest pain" or "I can't breathe"), the system:
1. Immediately displays emergency contact information
2. Prompts: "This sounds urgent. Do you need emergency help?"
3. If the patient confirms: connects to emergency services and notifies the care team
4. If the patient declines: the message is delivered to the clinician with an urgency flag
5. The clinician sees the urgency flag prominently in their message queue

This behavior is **always on** and cannot be configured away (I-019).

## 5. Relationship to external notifications

The in-app inbox is the **system of record** for all notifications. External channels (WhatsApp, SMS) are delivery mechanisms — they push the patient to open the app.

| Scenario | External notification | Inbox behavior |
|---|---|---|
| Clinician sends a message | WhatsApp notification: "Dr. [Name] sent you a message" | Full message visible in inbox |
| System sends a status update | WhatsApp/SMS per Notification Spec channel hierarchy | Full update visible in inbox |
| AI conversation escalation | WhatsApp notification: "Your care team has been notified" | Escalation record visible in inbox |
| Patient sends a message | No external notification to patient | Message visible in sent items |

External notifications **never contain clinical detail**. The notification says "You have a new message from your care team" — the patient must open the app to read the content. This protects PHI and is especially important for shared-device scenarios common in Ghana.

---

## 6. Delegation and messaging

When a delegate is acting on behalf of a patient:
- Delegate can read messages in threads they have scope to access
- Delegate can send messages on behalf of the patient — the message is attributed to the delegate (I-018): "Sent by [Delegate Name] on behalf of [Patient Name]"
- Sensitive-category threads (mental health, sexual health, substance use, reproductive health) are **not visible to delegates** by default (ADR-009)
- The patient can explicitly grant delegate access to sensitive threads

## 7. Attachments

| Attachment type | Supported | Max size | Safety check |
|---|---|---|---|
| Photos | Yes | 10 MB | Scanned for inappropriate content |
| Lab documents (PDF) | Yes | 20 MB | Routed to lab interpretation pipeline |
| Medication photos | Yes | 10 MB | Routed to medication identification |
| Video | No (at launch) | — | — |
| Audio | No (at launch) | — | — |

Attachments are stored securely and linked to the message. They are included in data export if the patient requests their data.

---

## 8. Read receipts

| Direction | Read receipts | Delivery receipts |
|---|---|---|
| Patient → Clinician | Clinician sees when patient read their message | Yes |
| Clinician → Patient | Patient sees "Sent" and "Read" status | Yes |
| Patient → Support | Support sees read status | Yes |
| System → Patient | System tracks open rate | No read receipt shown to patient |

Read receipts are **not available for AI conversations** — Mode 1 always "reads" immediately.

---

## 9. Message retention and export

- Clinical messages (patient ↔ clinician): retained per Category A audit rules (minimum 10 years)
- All other messages: retained per Category C rules (per data-use consent and jurisdiction)
- Patient data export includes all messages they sent and received
- Deleted messages: patients can hide messages from their view, but messages are **not deleted from the audit trail** (I-003)

---

## 10. Inbox architecture

The patient inbox is a unified view combining:
1. **Conversations** — active threads with unread indicators, ordered by most recent activity
2. **Notifications** — system messages (refill status, lab results, reminders), ordered chronologically
3. **Archive** — closed threads and past notifications, searchable

The inbox supports:
- Filtering by type (clinical, pharmacy, support, system)
- Search by keyword within messages
- Unread count badge on the app tab bar
